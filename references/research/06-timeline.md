## Intellectual turning points

1. **1976 Tangshan earthquake** — source of his lifelong preoccupation with fragility, tail risk, and the moral weight of survival. He references it in both the memoir and in his modernization essays.
2. **1989 Tiananmen** — forces exile; ends his engineering/activist path; the rupture that sends him to Columbia.
3. **1993 Buffett lecture at Columbia** — the single conversion moment. He describes walking in skeptical (a Chinese refugee suspicious of Wall Street) and walking out decided to become a value investor. This is his own canonical origin story.
4. **1997** — founds Himalaya, initially long/short and Asia-focused, before concentrating.
5. **2002 BYD** — his first "circle of competence" China industrial bet; the idea that crystallizes his edge-through-local-knowledge thesis.
6. **2003 Thanksgiving meeting with Munger** — shifts him from long/short + event-driven toward Munger-style concentrated, quality-first, buy-and-hold investing. He has said publicly this was the single largest upgrade to his thinking.
7. **~2004 restructuring** — accepting Munger family capital forces him to adopt a Berkshire-style structure (no redemptions, permanent capital orientation, very few ideas).
8. **2010 Poor Charlie's Almanack foreword** — first time he publicly frames himself as the "third generation" of value investing (Graham → Buffett/Munger → Li Lu / globalized value investing).
9. **2014–2018 modernization essays** — shift from pure investor to civilizational theorist; he frames China and modernization as an irreversible paradigm shift, which then justifies long-duration equity ownership globally.
10. **2023-11 Munger's death** — visible tonal shift: more reflective, more focused on legacy/teaching, more willing to speak publicly in China.
11. **2024-12 Peking University keynote** — "The macro is what we must accept; the micro is where we can and should make a difference." Represents a consolidated late-career statement in the post-Munger, post-zero-rate, deglobalization-anxiety environment.

## View evolution / updates

- **Pre-2003**: eclectic — long/short, activist/event, distressed. Munger partnership ends this.
- **Post-2004**: concentrated long-only, permanent-capital mindset, quality-first.
- **2010s**: expands circle from China-only to truly global, with Google/BAC positions.
- **2014 onward**: layers on a civilizational/modernization frame — macro acceptance, micro differentiation.
- **Post-2023 (Munger's death)**: tone shifts from pupil to steward; more teaching, more public appearances in China specifically.
- **2024-12 keynote and 2025 book edition**: explicitly addresses post-zero-rate, deflation-risk, AI, and deglobalization — he is now arguing value investing *still* works in a fractured world, not just in the Pax-Americana era that defined Buffett's career. This is the most recent documented evolution.

## Notes on dating uncertainty

- **Birth year**: consistently 1966; exact date 1966-04-06 widely reported but not on a primary Himalaya/Columbia page. Treat year as firm, day as SECONDARY.
- **Columbia triple degree completion**: usually cited as 1996, but some sources say 1996 BA + 1996 JD/MBA vs. staggered dates. "Mid-1990s" is safest; 1996 is the most commonly reported year.
- **Himalaya founding**: 1997 is the canonical date but the firm was restructured in 2004 after Munger's capital came in — some sources confuse "founded" with "current form founded." The 1997 date is correct for original founding.
- **BYD first buy**: "2002" universally reported but exact month unclear without HKEX disclosure pull. Confidence: high on year, low on month.
- **Munger's first investment with Li Lu**: usually dated 2004 but the relationship began Thanksgiving 2003 — the gap between meeting and capital commitment is a few months.
- **Book publication**: Chinese first edition is commonly listed as 2020-05; some sources say April. Revised/expanded edition is spring 2025 (Sina Finance 2025-04-21 announcement).
